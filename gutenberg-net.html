The Gutenberg - Internet analogy

Based on a HN comment.

There is now a very entertaining python3 script at this gist, author 4lhc.

The idea is that there is an analogy between the historical events after the invention of the printing press and the recent events after the ARPANET.

Based on empirical observations, we propose the hypothesis:

(Δ t-historic) = (Δ t-today)^(log 5/ log 2)

where the Δ t-historic is the time in decades from the invention of the printing press and Δ t-today is the time in decades from the opening of the ARPANET.

 

 

Here is a list of interesting correspondences:

  • Gutenberg press (1436) - - - ARPANET (1969)
  • The Ninety-five Theses by Martin Luther (1517) - - - The WWW spreads (1994)
  • East India Company (1600) - - - Google moves to Mountain View (2003)
  • East India Company had 23 factories (1647) - - - Google buys Youtube (2006)
  • East India Company rule in India (1757) - - - Google buys Waze and DeepMind Technologies (2013-2014)
  • Lloyd's Coffee House (1686) - - - Bitcoin (2009)
  • Leibniz-Newton calculus controversy (1711) - - - Open Access fashion (2009)
  • [proposed by 4lhc] First major uprising against the East India Company (1857) - - - Google kills its AI ethics board after employee protests (2019)

Predictions: if there is anything serious about this analogy then we should observe some other analogies

  • East India Stock Dividend Redemption Act (1873) - - - Google declines (2020)
  • Einstein' Annus Mirabilis (1905) - - - First great scientific advance after the dark period of baby-boomers (2021)
  • ARPANET (1969) - - - the Net takes a new direction, freed from the bad influence of the old generation influencers (2024)
